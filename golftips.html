Golf Tips

Choose the Right Club

On holes with a tight fairway, sometimes it’s better to leave the driver in the bag. Try a 3 or 4 iron off the tee instead.

Master the Short Game

Your driver might get the attention, but your wedge and putter do the scoring. Spend extra time practicing chips, pitches, and putts.

Have a Go-To Shot

Whether it’s a punch-out, a knockdown, or a reliable fade, having one shot you trust under pressure can be a round-saver.

Think One Shot Ahead

Always consider your next shot when planning your current one. It’s better to leave yourself a full swing from 100 yards than a tricky 45-yard half shot.
